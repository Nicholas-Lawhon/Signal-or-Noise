## Stop Conditions

- A finding suggests changing the product name, locked positioning, launch
  offer, game rules, or another product-shaping commitment.
- Work requires selecting or purchasing an analytics/provider plan, accepting a
  legally sensitive consent approach, or approving collection of personal data.
- Work requires a support address, legal entity, counsel-approved policy text,
  external account creation, contacting testers or third parties, publishing,
  or spend.
- A required availability source cannot be checked without credentials or paid
  access, or a likely brand conflict needs qualified legal review.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. `business/foundation/positioning.md` contains one positioning statement,
   message hierarchy, audience/persona briefs, claims guardrails, and ranked
   channel hypotheses for daily-puzzle and finance-curious acquisition wedges;
   all claims match `soul.md` and current product behavior.
2. `business/foundation/brand-screening.md` records the exact name, domain
   variants, social platforms/handles, trademark sources, search date, result,
   source link, and follow-up owner. It states that the work is preliminary
   research, and every required item has either no known blocker or an explicit
   user decision/legal-review stop.
3. `business/foundation/tester-recruitment.md` defines qualification, segment
   mix (including a less-finance-oriented control), sample targets, recruitment
   channels, screener, consent/privacy handling, incentive assumptions,
   conversion stages, feedback routing, and a no-outreach execution checklist.
4. `business/foundation/measurement-spec.md` defines canonical events and
   properties, acquisition/activation/retention funnels, attribution needs,
   consent and data-minimization requirements, data owner, reporting cadence,
   and a coverage matrix mapping each Stage 1/2 metric to an event or research
   instrument. It excludes pre-reveal answer data and unnecessary personal data.
5. `business/foundation/legal-support-readiness.md` tracks privacy, terms,
   disclaimer, support contact/workflow, refunds, and store disclosures with
   status, required input, owner, approval authority, and required-by milestone;
   no draft is represented as legal approval.
6. The five artifacts use one shared vocabulary, make no unsupported platform,
   price, release-date, availability, or legal claims, and collect unresolved
   approval items in one decision table rather than duplicating them.
7. A phase-boundary review confirms the Foundation exit gate: positioning and
   recruitment are ready for user approval; brand screening has no silent
   blocker; measurement and legal/support requirements are specific enough for
   separate implementation charters.
