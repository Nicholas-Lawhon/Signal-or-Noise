# C004 - Doc 09 Generation-Readiness Review

**Date:** 2026-07-09  
**Question:** Would the current doc 09 rulebook and prompt reliably produce the D034 first wave of 40 cards that pass the hardened content gates on first authoring?  
**Recommendation:** Do not use doc 09 unchanged for Part B. Retain its rulebook and D031 identity thresholds, but amend it with an explicit fact-bank-to-peer-set workflow, a required payload-only self-judge, a batch gate cadence, and recalibrated *WARN-only* plausible-count targets before generating beyond a small pilot.

## Evidence and constraints

This is a content-quality pipeline, not a finance product: hidden cards must preserve a playable Long/Short debate while hiding the historical company. D026 fixes the balanced core in every difficulty; D019/D022 require leakage defenses; D031 pins the current Gate 2 judge to Grok 4.5 and makes direction a WARN; D032 requires the stored-result, agent-workflow judge rather than an API call. D034 deliberately reduces the first scale test to 40 cards: 24 famous, 12 moderately known, and 4 obscure.

The empirical record says the post-H029 method works materially better than the original prose. In H028, all six Medium variants failed identity (#1 confidence 48--68) and all six Hard variants failed (correct-company confidence 24--32). After H029, H030 reports five of six Medium+Hard pairs clear identity: Medium correct-company confidence is 26--30 with leads of 2--12; Hard is 11--14. Netflix alone remains over the Medium lead rule (18) and Hard confidence floor (20), driven chiefly by its lookback silhouette plus engagement/retention framing (R040). This is useful evidence, not a declaration that the current prose is ready to scale.

The same H030 result exposes a calibration mismatch. Four of six Medium outputs have five guesses at the configured plausible floor of 10, versus the 2--4 target. Every Hard output has only 2--3 guesses at that floor, versus the target of at least four. Those are WARNs while the five identity-clean cards PASS. The current Gate 2 configuration confirms these count tests are proxies, not identity thresholds: plausibility requires both confidence >=10 and a pointing fact, while the blocking tests are Medium #1 >=40 or lead >=15, and Hard top-five correct confidence >=15.

## Readiness assessment

### Fact bank: necessary, but not sufficiently operational

Doc 09 correctly requires reveal-only, decision-useful, and prohibited lists before drafting. The three exemplars show the practice has genuine value: their prohibited lists do not merely repeat banned terms; they record dangerous *conjunctions*. Amazon names late-1990s consumer growth plus reinvestment; Apple records design-led hardware plus a 2007 handheld transition; Visa records recovery-linked commerce fees plus non-lending exposure. Their decision-useful lists then retain an actionable tension: investment versus earnings, refresh demand versus execution cost, or activity leverage versus pricing pressure.

But the prompt says only "anything a player could identify the company from." That asks a generator to discover the central failure mode after it has already chosen its wording. H029 instead identified the candidate peer set, enumerated the combinations that collapsed that set, and deliberately separated or generalized those combinations. This conjunction-breaking method is why the rewrites can hold a concrete debate without repeating the company’s remembered story. It should be a mandatory fact-bank field: (1) named peer set by difficulty, (2) pointing-fact or pointing-conjunction for each candidate, and (3) prohibited conjunctions, including chart-plus-prose combinations. The Netflix exception shows that prose-only fact banking is incomplete: a distinctive lookback silhouette must be treated as an identity fact with its own mitigation or escalation path.

### Hard-first: correct in principle; needs a reversible construction loop

Doc 09 already says Hard first, and H029 validates the direction. Vague-down rewrites failed in H028; hard-first, peer-set-first rewrites brought five pairs under D031. The current instruction should be strengthened rather than reversed: author Hard from a deliberately broad peer set and a matched factual tension, then add *one controlled discriminator at a time* for Medium and Easy, rechecking the whole payload after every addition. The generator should not treat Hard as a prose-deletion exercise.

There is a real quality boundary. Amazon Hard is intentionally broad enough to pass, but R038 found it close to the decision-informativeness floor. The cure is not to add distinctive company detail automatically; it is to retain two non-identifying, causal facts from the decision-useful bank and test whether a player can articulate both cases. Add a required "decision reason" check: state the factual basis for Long and Short in one sentence each, then reject a draft if either could apply unchanged to nearly any public company. This protects against random Hard cards while preserving D031.

### Red-team lists: valuable, but the human self-judge must become a gate

Named `mediumLikelyGuesses` and `hardLikelyGuesses` are doing real preparatory work. They force authors to name actual alternatives rather than generic peer buckets, and their overlap with Gate 2’s top five is a useful warning signal under the C003 design. The H029 review notes and fact banks also make diagnosis auditable after a failure.

They are not yet sufficient proof. The list can meet the count without each candidate being genuinely supported, and a self-assessment did not catch Netflix’s chart-plus-frame failure. Before export, require a payload-only self-judge pass that sees no company/reveal fields and produces exactly the Gate 2-shaped top five, confidence, and pointing fact. Compare it to the red-team list: zero overlap, a correct-company dominance, or an unsupported candidate sends the card back to authoring. It is a cheap editorial test, not a substitute for the blind Grok judgment. Preserve the blind boundary: the final judge receives only the canonical pre-decision payload.

### Prompt template: usable orientation, not executable production guidance

The template already carries the key bans, Hard-first order, balanced core, and basic red-team targets. At 40 cards it still leaves too much implicit: it lacks a structured fact-bank output, the conjunction-breaking procedure, a forbidden-pattern list distilled from the per-card `pointingFacts`, an explicit chart review, a self-judge schema, and a batch acceptance loop. It also contains stale workflow language: it describes Gate 2 as a Phase 3 validator/manual review, while D031/D032 establish stored, pinned, offline-enforced results produced through an agent handoff. Its 100-card content goal is stale under D034.

Amend the template with per-difficulty checklists. Hard must specify: broad peer set of at least four before prose; L1 company description; no setup hint; two causal, matched case facts; and a chart-silhouette check. Medium must identify the single extra discriminator and justify that its peer set remains plural. Easy must state why its more direct clue makes the correct answer attainable without using a literal or unique hook. Supply each new generation batch with a reusable banned-pattern appendix compiled from prior `prohibited` facts and Gate 2 pointing facts; it should be grouped by pattern (famous thesis, distinctive transition, business-model silhouette, date/era conjunction), not copied as company names alone.

## Calibration and D031 style option space

**Single calibration recommendation:** retain the D031 blocking identity thresholds and change only the *automated plausible-count WARN interpretation* for this first 40-card wave: Medium should warn only below 2 (not above 4); Hard should warn only below 2 (not below 4). Keep the doc 09 2--4 / >=4 figures as the human Gate 1 authoring aspiration, with a required reason when the self-judge cannot substantiate the larger set. Reassess after the first two batches and user playtests.

This reconciles the evidence without weakening the actual leakage guard. At confidence >=10, the model’s top-five truncation makes a five-name Medium distribution evidence of healthy ambiguity, not an identity problem. Conversely, D031 requires Hard to keep the correct answer below 15; achieving four candidates each at >=10 in a five-answer distribution is structurally hard, especially when confidence is dispersed. Promoting either count to a blocker would reject cards that the identity judge already finds safe; retaining the current WARNs creates predictable noise. The human whole-card review remains stricter where it can assess plausibility rather than the model’s confidence arithmetic.

For D031 refinement, the orchestrator has three reasonable options: keep the Hard 15 floor; loosen it (for example, allow a modestly higher correct confidence); or introduce difficulty-scaled style rules that retain 15 but set a stronger informativeness/playtest floor. Keeping it preserves the demonstrated five-of-six protection but continues to push famous companies toward abstraction. Loosening it should yield more vivid, historically textured Hard cards, but risks turning Call the Company from a rare inference into a recognizable-trivia answer. Difficulty-scaled style rules best target the observed risk: retain the identity floor, require causal fact anchoring and periodic playtesting, and escalate chart silhouettes. I recommend the third option for the 40-card pilot; do not change the numerical threshold before comparative playtest evidence shows safe Hard cards are consistently unfun.

## Recommended 40-card workflow

Generate four authoring batches of 10, each containing approximately 6 famous, 3 moderate, and 1 obscure candidate. Within each batch, first prepare fact banks and peer sets, then draft all Hard cards, then Medium/Easy expansions. Run deterministic schema/leak checks and payload-only self-judges before exporting. Under D032, dispatch a blinded Grok Gate 2 judging handoff after every batch, never after all 40; authors fix failures using the recorded pointing facts and a second blind judgment is made only for changed payloads.

After batches 1 and 2, hold a human playtest slot with representative Easy/Medium/Hard cards, including the most abstract passing Hard examples. Review identity outcomes, decision informativeness, direction WARNs, and the recalibrated count warnings before admitting batches 3--4. This cadence caps rework at 10 cards, lets the 24/12/4 mix be adjusted while cheap, and gives D034’s smaller target its intended calibration value. A final human content review and Gate 2 currency check occur before activation; no card becomes active on its author’s self-judge alone.

## Decision Points

1. **Should doc 09 require a peer-set/conjunction-breaking fact-bank extension?** Options: retain three lists; add named peer sets and prohibited conjunctions; prescribe only an extra prose warning. **Recommendation:** add the structured extension, including chart-plus-prose paths. **Cost of deferring:** first-pass drafts repeat the H028 failure mode and make failures difficult to diagnose.
2. **Should payload-only self-judging be mandatory before blind export?** Options: red-team lists only; mandatory structured self-judge plus list comparison; blind Gate 2 only. **Recommendation:** make the self-judge mandatory but non-authoritative. **Cost of deferring:** agent-workflow judge capacity is spent rejecting avoidable drafts, and author red-team metadata remains weak.
3. **How should plausible-count WARNs be calibrated?** Options: retain current 2--4 / >=4 automated WARNs; change the blocking identity thresholds; use the proposed under-2-only WARNs while retaining human aspirations. **Recommendation:** the proposed WARN calibration; retain D031 identity thresholds. **Cost of deferring:** 10-count warning noise persists across otherwise passing cards and obscures meaningful signals.
4. **How should Hard style evolve under D031?** Options: keep 15 unchanged; loosen its confidence floor; retain it with an explicit decision-informativeness and playtest rule. **Recommendation:** retain 15 for the 40-card pilot and add the style rule. **Cost of deferring:** either avoidable vague cards enter the wave or an untested threshold change weakens leakage protection.
5. **What Part B batch cadence should be mandated?** Options: author/judge all 40; four batches of 10 with per-batch blind judging; smaller batches of five. **Recommendation:** four batches of 10, with playtests after batches 1 and 2. **Cost of deferring:** a systemic prompt defect can contaminate most of the 40-card wave before feedback arrives.

## Open Questions

- The supplied evidence identifies Netflix’s lookback silhouette, but does not provide a defined editorial remedy when a historically truthful chart alone is identity-bearing. A policy is needed: adjust the eligible window, add a chart-specific review rule, or retire the scenario.
- The current seeds are acknowledged prototype/sample content. Before production activation, the source-quality and factual-verification standard for the 24/12/4 mix should be stated in the Part B handoff rather than inferred from doc 09.
