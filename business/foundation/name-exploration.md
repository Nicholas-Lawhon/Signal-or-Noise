## Naming criteria

A strong name should feel like a daily puzzle or trivia game, support the hidden
company and reveal loop, leave room for historical business stories, and avoid
brokerage, gambling, live-signal, or financial-advice implications. Prefer a
distinctive phrase that is easy to say, spell, search, and extend across web,
iOS, Android, support email, and social handles.

## Initial candidates

| Candidate | Why it works | Watch-out |
|---|---|---|
| **Call It History** | Punchy, game-like, and ties “make the call” to historical truth. | Could sound like a general history product without the descriptor. |
| **You Had the Clues** | Captures the core tension and satisfying hindsight reveal. | Longer name; needs concise handle treatment. |
| **In Plain Hindsight** | Cleverly combines hidden clues with hindsight and feels puzzle-first. | “Hindsight” is used by other products and a separate game, so clearance matters. |
| **Hindsight Hunt** | Short, playful, and naturally supports a daily challenge. | May overemphasize identity hunting instead of Long/Short/Pass. |
| **Company Unmasked** | Immediately explains the company-reveal payoff. | More descriptive and potentially less ownable. |
| **Past the Bell** | Compact market-history flavor without sounding like a trading terminal. | Could be mistaken for after-hours market news. |
| **The Hidden Outcome** | Broad puzzle framing that fits incomplete-information decisions. | Does not communicate companies or history on its own. |
| **Before the Reveal** | Directly names the dramatic moment the game is built around. | Generic phrase; may be hard to own in search. |
| **History on the Line** | Suggests a consequential call while keeping the subject historical. | “On the line” can carry betting or telephone associations. |
| **The Reveal Test** | Clear daily-game energy and easy campaign framing. | Generic and less tied to company history. |

Recommended first shortlist: **Call It History**, **You Had the Clues**, and
**In Plain Hindsight**.

## Preliminary domain signal

On 2026-07-10, Verisign `.com` RDAP returned no domain object for
`callithistory.com`, `youhadtheclues.com`, `inplainhindsight.com`,
`hindsighthunt.com`, `companyunmasked.com`, and `pastthebell.com`. A no-object
response is not proof that a domain is purchasable or free of rights; registrar,
premium/reserved-name, common-law, app-store, social, and trademark checks still
apply immediately before any registration.
