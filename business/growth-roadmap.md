# Signal or Noise? Growth Roadmap

## Goal and Operating Model

Turn Signal or Noise? from a working MVP into a sustainable game business across
one integrated web presence plus iOS and Android apps. The website is both the
playable web product and the acquisition hub, with platform-aware paths to play
on web or install the mobile apps.

The broad top-of-funnel audience is daily-puzzle players. The first reachable
wedge is finance-curious gamers, trivia and fantasy-sports players, and people
who enjoy company and business history. Every channel must present the product
as an entertainment game—not trading, gambling, or financial advice.

The Growth Owner owns strategy, messaging, assets, campaigns, measurement, and
commercial experiments. Product or website code is implemented through a
separate approved charter. External publishing, outreach, account creation, and
spend follow the authority boundary in `agents/roles/growth.md`.

## Launch Offer

- Free Daily Challenge and a meaningful Classic sampler.
- One-time Premium Unlock for the full core game.
- Premium belongs to an authenticated account and restores across web, iOS, and
  Android; official leaderboards remain fair and never sell scoring advantages.
- Web checkout and store-native mobile purchases feed one server-owned entitlement.
  Apple and Google billing, restoration, pricing, tax, and regional rules must be
  rechecked immediately before implementation and store submission.
- Paid scenario packs are the first expansion after launch. Cosmetics, advanced
  stats, subscriptions, and in-product ads remain later evidence-gated options.

Launch price is selected during private-beta willingness-to-pay testing and must
be approved before products are configured in any storefront.

## Growth Stages

### 1. Foundation

**Owner:** Growth Owner
**Entry:** Phase 8 accepted; core positioning and locked product rules available.
**Dependencies:** `soul.md`, business plan, current product behavior.

**Deliverables**

- Positioning one-pager, message hierarchy, audience/persona briefs, and channel
  hypotheses for daily-puzzle and finance-curious segments.
- Name, domain, social-handle, and preliminary trademark screening; findings are
  research, not legal clearance.
- Private-tester recruitment plan and screener spanning puzzle players,
  finance-curious gamers, trivia players, and less-finance-oriented controls.
- Measurement specification: event names, funnel definitions, attribution needs,
  consent/privacy requirements, reporting cadence, and data owner.
- Legal/support checklist covering privacy, terms, disclaimer, support contact,
  refund handling, and store disclosures.

**Metrics:** qualified tester pool, recruitment conversion, channel response,
landing-page intent signals, and measurement coverage.
**Exit gate:** positioning and recruitment plan approved; required names/handles
have no known blocker; analytics and legal requirements are implementation-ready.

### 2. Product Readiness and Private Beta

**Owner:** Growth Owner, with separate Phase 9/mobile implementation owners.
**Entry:** Foundation exit gate passed.
**Dependencies:** Phase 9 polish, analytics implementation, Expo readiness, store
accounts, support/legal inputs, and beta builds.

**Deliverables**

- Integrated website requirements for acquisition, web play, app-install calls to
  action, FAQ, rules, support, privacy, terms, and financial-advice disclaimer.
- App Store and Play Store title/subtitle, descriptions, keywords, screenshot
  plan, preview storyboard, icon requirements, and review notes.
- Share-card copy and creative specification, press/creator kit, approved social
  templates, email/waitlist flow, and launch FAQ.
- TestFlight, Play closed-test, and web-beta recruitment/feedback plan.
- Paywall concepts and Premium Unlock price research without activating billing.

**Metrics:** landing-to-play/install intent, onboarding completion, first-run
completion, scoring comprehension, Daily completion, account conversion, D1/D7
return, share intent/use, paywall comprehension, and willingness to pay.
**Exit gate:** critical beta issues resolved; analytics events reconcile; store,
website, support, and campaign assets are approved; launch price and free/premium
boundary are approved; web/iOS/Android release candidates are ready.

### 3. Coordinated Web, iOS, and Android Launch

**Owner:** Growth Owner within an approved campaign envelope.
**Entry:** Product-readiness exit gate passed.
**Dependencies:** production releases, purchase/restore verification, store
approval, website readiness, support coverage, and approved budget.

**Deliverables**

- Coordinated release calendar and rollback/communications plan across all three
  platforms.
- Approved organic social calendar, creator/community outreach, email launch,
  SEO/ASO pages, and selected launch-directory or press submissions.
- Small capped acquisition tests with channel, audience, creative, attribution,
  stop-loss, and success criteria defined before spend.
- Daily launch dashboard and issue-routing process for product, billing, support,
  reputation, and campaign problems.

**Metrics:** qualified traffic, installs, cost per activated player, first-run and
Daily completion, D1/D7 retention, account conversion, paywall views, Premium
conversion, refunds, net revenue, sharing, and support burden.
**Exit gate:** launch incident window closed; attribution and revenue reconcile;
baseline cohorts are large enough to select the next retention/conversion tests.

### 4. Retention and Conversion Optimization

**Owner:** Growth Owner; product experiments receive implementation charters.
**Entry:** Stable launch baseline available.
**Dependencies:** reliable cohort reporting and an experiment delivery cadence.

**Deliverables**

- Weekly ranked experiment backlog across acquisition message, onboarding, first
  reveal, Daily habit, account creation, sharing, paywall, and reactivation.
- Cohort review segmented by platform, source, new/returning status, and free or
  premium access without exposing private player data.
- Creative and channel iteration based on activated and retained players—not raw
  clicks or installs alone.
- Approved lifecycle messaging plan once notification/email permissions and
  product support exist.

**Metrics:** D1/D7/D30 retention, weekly retained Daily players, runs per player,
Daily and Classic completion, account and Premium conversion, share/referral
rate, churn/refunds, CAC, contribution margin, and payback.
**Exit gate:** repeatable retention and conversion gains exist; scalable channels
meet approved unit-economics targets; content-pack demand is validated.

### 5. Paid Scenario Packs

**Owner:** Growth Owner with Content Curator and product implementation owners.
**Entry:** Optimization exit gate passed.
**Dependencies:** content capacity, entitlement/catalog design, store products,
purchase restoration, merchandising surfaces, and support/refund readiness.

**Deliverables**

- Prioritized pack themes based on player demand and content feasibility.
- Pack positioning, pricing research, merchandising, launch calendar, creator
  angles, and free-to-paid sampling strategy.
- Fairness rules that keep paid content additive and exclude paid advantages from
  official comparable leaderboards unless access is equalized.

**Metrics:** pack-page views, attach and purchase rate, revenue per payer, repeat
purchase, content completion, refunds, retention lift, and support burden.
**Exit gate:** at least one pack demonstrates sustainable demand and acceptable
content/support economics, or the model is explicitly retired or revised.

### 6. Revenue Expansion

**Owner:** Growth Owner.
**Entry:** Core Premium Unlock and pack economics are understood.
**Dependencies:** retention, player research, implementation capacity, legal/store
review, and approved experiments.

**Deliverables**

- Evidence-backed evaluation of cosmetics, advanced stats, subscription/season
  access, and in-product ads.
- Explicit pay-to-win, privacy, UX, brand, and leaderboard-integrity review for
  every candidate before implementation.
- Scale plan for paid acquisition only when contribution margin and payback meet
  approved targets.

**Metrics:** incremental revenue and margin, conversion, retention impact, churn,
ad or subscription burden, sentiment, and payback.
**Exit gate:** user approves the next business model and its implementation
charter, or the current Premium-plus-packs model remains the deliberate default.

## Scorecard

- **Acquisition:** qualified visitors, installs, source attribution, campaign
  spend, cost per activated player.
- **Activation:** play starts, first decision, first reveal, first-run completion,
  scoring comprehension.
- **Retention:** Daily participation/completion, D1/D7/D30 return, weekly retained
  Daily players, runs per player.
- **Engagement and sharing:** leaderboard participation, share-card use, referrals,
  creator/community response.
- **Revenue:** paywall views, Premium and pack conversion, refunds, net revenue,
  revenue per payer, CAC, contribution margin, and payback.

Exact targets are approved after beta establishes a trustworthy baseline. Paid
acquisition may test before then only with a small capped learning budget and may
not scale until retention and payback targets are approved.
